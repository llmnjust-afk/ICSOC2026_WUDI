\section{Background and Related Work}
\label{sec:background}

\subsection{Average versus marginal carbon intensity}
The carbon intensity of grid electricity is not a single number: the \emph{average} intensity records the average electron, while the \emph{marginal} intensity records the next electron~\cite{sukprasert2024implications}. Because the marginal generator is typically fossil and the average mix contains zero-carbon baseload, the two signals differ, often by a factor of two or three, and they are poorly correlated in the hours that matter for scheduling. Sukprasert et al.\ show that using the average signal systematically misranks deferral windows and can increase emissions; the Green Mirage result extends this to estimation methods~\cite{maji2024green}. The sunk-carbon critique argues that embodied carbon must be charged when a deferral triggers a cold start~\cite{bashir2024sunk}. Figure~\ref{fig:marginal-vs-avg} illustrates the divergence: the marginal signal swings sharply between fossil-ramp peaks and renewable-surplus troughs, while the average signal lags and compresses both, so the two signals recommend different deferral targets.

\subsection{Carbon-aware workload shifting}
The carbon-aware shifting literature has matured rapidly. Asadov et al.\ review the field but use the average signal~\cite{asadov2025carbonaware}. Patel et al.\ argue for an agile pathway to carbon-aware clouds on the marginal signal in principle but stop short of an online scheduler~\cite{patel2024agile}. Radhakrishnan uses DRL for Kubernetes workloads but treats jobs as coarse-grained batches rather than per-invocation FaaS~\cite{radhakrishnan2024carbonaware}. Caribou shifts serverless applications geospatially between regions using marginal intensity but does not model temporal deferral across a surplus window~\cite{gsteiger2024caribou}. Lechowicz et al.\ give learning-augmented competitive algorithms for spatiotemporal online allocation with deadlines~\cite{lechowicz2024learningaugmented}, the closest theoretical neighbour, but they do not couple the marginal signal to per-invocation embodied amortisation.

\subsection{Continuum and cold starts}
Cloud--edge continuum modelling work~\cite{patel2024modeling,aldulaimy2024computing} formalises the heterogeneity of the continuum, and service-placement work~\cite{rac2024costaware,soumplis2024performance} formalises the cost of being in the wrong tier. The cold-start literature provides the reuse decision that drives embodied amortisation: RainbowCake caches layers~\cite{yu2024rainbowcake}, CodeCrunch predicts invocations and warmups \cite{roy2024codecrunch}, and cross-edge caching places functions probabilistically across edges~\cite{chen2024crossedge}. None of these threads charges the cold-start decision with a carbon cost. \textsc{CarbonShift} bridges this gap by consuming the marginal signal online, exposing the embodied term through the warm-reuse decision, and providing theoretical guarantees against a marginal-aware offline optimum.
